Bread maker recipes and experiments.

Review the default programs, then log your own bread maker creations and notes.

Operation (important)

  • Always place liquids and salt first, then dry ingredients, and finally yeast on top.
  • Make a small indentation in the dry ingredients for the yeast.
  • Yeast must not touch liquids or salt until the program starts.
  • Follow the recipe order and use the exact ingredient quantities.
  • No recipes are listed for programs 8 (Bake), 11 (Mix Stir), or 13 (Home Made).

Hints & tips

  • Center the appliance on the counter so it does not walk during kneading.
  • Use a scale for better measurement accuracy.
  • Remove bread right after baking to avoid a dark crust or soggy loaf.
  • Avoid opening the lid during rising or baking to prevent collapse.
  • If the dough is too wet, add flour 1 tsp at a time; if too dry, add liquid 1 tsp at a time.
  • At high altitude, reduce yeast by 1/4–1/2 tsp if the loaf rises too high.
  • Let cold ingredients warm to room temperature before adding them.
  • Warm liquids and butter gently on defrost so they do not overheat.
  • A pinch of powdered ginger or vitamin C can help yeast activity.
  • Whole wheat loaves are denser and shorter than white flour breads.
Cleaning & maintenance: Clean the appliance after every use. Let all parts cool completely, then wipe down the bread pan and exterior. Leave servicing or repairs to qualified personnel.
Warning: Always switch off and unplug the appliance before cleaning.
